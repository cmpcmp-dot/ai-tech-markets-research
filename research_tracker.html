About
Purpose

This tracker is a curated research intelligence tool at the intersection of artificial intelligence, automation, and labor markets. It aggregates peer-reviewed studies, policy reports, and investigative journalism into a structured database — and synthesizes that research into strategic themes and policy frameworks — to support institutional positioning, workforce strategy, and policy analysis.

Created by

Christelle Prophete. This site is independently maintained and updated as new research emerges. The content does not reflect the opinions or perspectives of the creator.

Research tab

A filterable card database of individual research entries. Each entry is tagged by category, geographic scope, and recency status — Emergent (under 90 days, preliminary), Current (3–18 months, active in the research conversation), or Stale (18+ months, useful as baseline but context may have shifted). Cards can be filtered by category, status, and geography, or searched by title, source, or finding.

Themes tab

Cross-cutting syntheses of the research database, organized into eight themes. Each theme frames a key strategic question, presents an evidence consensus, identifies implications for institutional actors, and notes gaps in the current evidence base. Themes carry an editorial confidence rating: Strong consensus, Emerging evidence, or Contested.

Policy Lab tab

Twenty-five policy interventions organized by category and mapped to five stages of AI-driven labor disruption — from Foundational (reforms needed regardless of AI's trajectory) through Transformational (responses to large-scale displacement of the workforce). Each policy card includes a plain-language description, an account of the implementation challenge, and links to supporting research in the database. Does not constitute policy advice.

AI Disclaimer

Summaries, key findings, and takeaways are AI-generated and reviewed for accuracy on an ongoing basis. They are intended as orientation aids — not substitutes for reading the original source. Always consult the primary publication before citing or acting on any finding.
