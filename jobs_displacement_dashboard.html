01 · The aggregate

Is unemployment higher than growth alone would predict?

Okun’s law ties the change in unemployment to how fast the economy grows. If unemployment were rising faster than growth predicts, that would point to something structural, an AI-driven effect on top of the ordinary cycle. Each point is a quarter; the line is the historical relationship; the last four quarters are traced in sequence.

Okun’s law: GDP growth vs. change in unemployment

Low unemployment can also hide withdrawal. The companion check is prime-age (25–54) participation, which strips out retirement; if it were falling, the headline would be flattering.
